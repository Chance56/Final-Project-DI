Thomas Alva Edison

The man who developed Direct Current

Here's a timeline of Thomas Edison's life:

  • 1847 - Born in Milan, Ohio, but grew up in Port Huron,Michigan, USA
  • 1854 -Edison receives minimal formal education but demonstrates an early interest in science and technology.
  • 1866 - At age 19, Edison moved to Louisville, Kentucky as an employee of Western Union.
  • 1868 - Edison secures his first patent for an electric vote recorder, although it does not gain commercial success.
  • 1876 - Edison establishes a laboratory in Menlo Park, New Jersey, which becomes the birthplace of many of his significant inventions.
  • 1877 - Edison invents the phonograph, a device that can record and play back sound. This invention brings him international fame.
  • 1879 - Edison successfully develops a practical and long-lasting incandescent electric light bulb. This achievement revolutionizes the way people live and work.
  • 1882 - Edison helps establish the Edison Electric Illuminating Company of New York, one of the first electric utility companies.
  • 1887 - Edison moves his operations to West Orange, New Jersey, where he constructs a larger research facility called the "Invention Factory." This facility focuses on multiple areas of research and development.
  • 1891 - Edison receives a patent for the kinetoscope, a motion picture camera. He later invents the kinetophone, a device that combines motion pictures with synchronized sound.
  • 1897 - His first patent was for the electric vote recorder, U.S. Patent 90,646, which was granted on June 1, 1869
  • 1903 - Edison launches the first commercially successful alkaline storage battery, known as the Edison Storage Battery. It finds applications in various fields, including electric vehicles.
  • 1914 - Edison's research efforts concentrate on developing new materials for the production of synthetic rubber during World War I."
  • 1928 - Edison introduces the first practical motion picture sound system, known as the "Movietone." This innovation revolutionizes the film industry.
  • 1931 - Edison's wife, Mina, passes away.
  • October 18,1931 - Thomas Edison dies at the age of 84 in West Orange, New Jersey.
Throughout his lifetime, Edison obtained over 1,000 patents for his inventions, which spanned a wide range of fields, including telegraphy, telephony, electrical power, motion pictures, and more. His contributions significantly impacted modern technology and laid the groundwork for many innovations that followed.
